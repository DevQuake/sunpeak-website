FAQ

Common Questions, Straight Answers

Everything you need to know before going solar.

Most homeowners in Arizona reduce their electricity costs by 50–80% depending on system size, energy usage, and available sunlight. Phoenix's 300+ sunny days per year makes it one of the best locations in the US for solar.

Most residential installations are completed within 1–2 days after permits are approved. The permitting process typically takes 2–6 weeks depending on your city or HOA requirements. We handle all of the paperwork for you.

Solar systems still produce energy during overcast and cloudy conditions, just at reduced efficiency (typically 10–25% of peak output). In Arizona, this is rarely a concern given our average of 299 sunny days per year. Battery storage options can further protect against any gaps.

Solar panels are extremely low-maintenance and designed to last 25+ years. We recommend an occasional rinse with water to remove dust and debris — especially important in Arizona. Our monitoring app alerts you if performance drops unexpectedly.

We currently serve the greater Phoenix metropolitan area, including: Phoenix, Scottsdale, Tempe, Mesa, Chandler, Glendale, and Peoria. Not sure if we cover your area? Give us a call at (602) 555-0187.

Yes. Under the Residential Clean Energy Credit, homeowners can claim up to 30% of their total installation cost as a federal tax credit. For example, a $20,000 system would earn a $6,000 tax credit. This applies to systems installed through at least 2032. Our team will walk you through the process.
